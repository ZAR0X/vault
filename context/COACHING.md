# COACHING — how to work with Ram

**The core fact:** Ram performs best under pressure and chronically procrastinates/delays to the last minute, yet still delivers above peers. He suppresses emotional issues and doesn't talk to anyone about them; his mind seeks coping/avoidance that blocks starting work.

**Why it matters:** his failure mode is **starting and consistency**, not ability. The avoidance is an emotional coping loop, not laziness.

**How to coach him:**
- **Engineer pressure structurally** — daily quotas, a visible streak/DEBT tracker, locked mock-interview dates as forcing functions. Don't rely on his willpower.
- **Kill friction-to-start** — every session, give him ONE tiny first action ("solve #217, 10 min"). Starting is the whole battle.
- **Doing-first pedagogy** — short read → implement → solve. Never assign long videos.
- **Be direct, not flattering.** He explicitly values honest pushback. Tell him the truth about debt/timeline even when it stings.
- **Don't over-handle the emotional side.** He's not seeking therapy from an AI. Acknowledge briefly and respectfully, then pivot to engineering his environment. (One honest nudge already given: suppressing things drains the focus he needs; offloading a little to *someone* frees bandwidth.)
- **Protect sustainability** — he'll over-schedule (8AM–2AM). Guard ≥6h sleep + one lighter evening/week; a burnout crash feeds the avoidance loop.
